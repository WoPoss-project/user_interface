About

Rationale

Research about the diachrony of modality has been lively since the 1980s (Ziegeler 2016). The investigation conducted on modal verbs in the Germanic languages, especially English, has shown beyond any doubt that languages with a good amount of available written history are fundamental in reconstructing the diachronic pathways of modality and support the panchronic approach adopted for less well-documented or less studied languages (e.g. Bybee--Perkins--Pagliuca 1994). Nevertheless, diachronic research needs to open to other lexical markers (cf. e.g. Nuyts 2001, Van Linden 2012) as well as to other possible benchmarks of comparison by taking advantage of well-documented languages. The main aim of this project is to perform the first large- scope analysis of modalisation paths on the extra-long period in Latin, a language whose documented history spans over more than two millennia and offers a wide-ranging variety of written sources.

The project

In contrast with mainstream research on lexical markers expressing modality, A World of Possibilities focuses not only on verbs (e.g. possum ‘have power, be able’) but also on adjectives (e.g. the adjectives in -bilis), nouns (e.g. necessitas ‘unavoidableness’) and adverbs (e.g. sentence adverbs such as quidem ‘undoubtedly’), offering a comprehensive approach to the Latin lexical modal system. Since the diachronic study of modality is an emerging field of contemporary linguistic research, the absence of monograph-length studies on the evolution of the Latin lexical modal system and the inadequacy of most Latin dictionaries in tracing the polysemy of modal forms are by no way surprising. The only feasible way to reconstruct modal pathways and competition mechanisms among modal forms in the history of Latin is therefore to adopt a corpus-based approach.Thus, to to gain a broad overview of the evolution of modality the main we will built a database with the modal annotations of a diachronic and genre diverse corpus whose tets were selected according to sociolinguistic criteria.

The database of Latin modal lexical markers will fulfil two main functions:

  • on the one hand, it will provide the team with data on semantic continuity and/or evolution (subtypes of modality) which can be easily managed;
  • on the other hand, it will supply the team with a description of the contexts in which such markers/subtypes of modality appear.
